# UPI Scam Safety Playbook

This quick playbook helps users react safely when a message is flagged as risky.

## 1. Stop and Verify

- Do not approve payment requests in panic.
- Verify through official app/helpdesk numbers only.
- Ignore phone numbers or links inside suspicious messages.

## 2. Never Share Secrets

Never share:

- OTP
- UPI PIN
- CVV
- Card PIN
- Net banking password

Banks and payment platforms do not ask these over chat, call, or random links.

## 3. Handle Suspicious Links Safely

- Do not click shortened links (for example: bit.ly, tinyurl).
- Open your bank/UPI app directly from your phone, not from message links.
- Check URL spelling carefully before entering credentials.

## 4. If You Already Shared Details

- Immediately block or freeze bank account/cards if needed.
- Reset UPI PIN/password from official app.
- Contact bank fraud helpline and report the incident.
- Report cybercrime through official government channels in your country.

## 5. Message Triage Cheat Sheet

High-risk indicators:

- Urgency: "act now", "final warning", "within 10 minutes"
- Reward bait: "lottery", "cash prize", "claim reward"
- Credential request: "share OTP", "confirm PIN", "enter password"
- Link pressure: "click here", "verify now", short links

If multiple indicators appear together, treat the message as potentially fraudulent.

## 6. For Teams and Educators

- Use this repository as an explainable demo for scam awareness sessions.
- Keep keyword list updated with latest scam wording.
- Pair this tool with human review and official escalation SOPs.
